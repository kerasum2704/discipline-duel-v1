## What It Does

The Discipline Duel turns the vague idea of self-control into something you can actually play. Rather than reading productivity advice, you step into a game where your own mindset becomes the arena. Each round throws a common excuse or mental block at you, and you fight back with a well-chosen "Counter-Strike." This playful approach helps you absorb discipline strategies while having a good time.

The game tracks two key stats: Resilience HP, which measures your ability to bounce back from setbacks, and your Future Focus Score, which shows how well you keep long-term goals in sight. As the duel progresses, the scenarios get tougher, mirroring real-life temptations and procrastination traps. You can replay as many times as you like, with each session revealing new patterns in your thinking and giving you practical comebacks to use in daily life.

## Getting Started

1. **Open the game** by visiting the URL in any modern web browser.
2. **No setup required** – everything runs client-side.
3. **Start a new duel** by clicking the "Begin" or "Start" button on the main screen.
4. **Follow the prompts** to respond to each excuse with your chosen Counter-Strike.

Example workflow:
```
1. Open https://jamesgabe.github.io/discipline-duel-v1/
2. Click "Start Duel"
3. Read the excuse (e.g., "I'm too tired to work today")
4. Select or type your Counter-Strike response
5. Watch your Resilience HP and Future Focus Score update
```

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do I start playing?**  
Just open the game URL in your browser and click the start button. No account, login, or download is needed.

**Can I save my progress?**  
Yes, progress is stored in your browser's local storage. Clearing your browser data will reset your scores.

**Can I customize the Counter-Strikes?**  
Absolutely – you can type your own responses instead of using the guided options.

**Does this work on mobile?**  
The game is fully responsive and runs on all modern mobile browsers.

**How often are new scenarios added?**  
The game uses a built-in library of excuses that randomizes each session. Future updates may introduce new content.
